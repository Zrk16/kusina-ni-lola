Since 1987

The story of
Lola Nena.

In 1987, Estrellita "Nena" Reyes set up a small clay pot and wood-fire stove in front of her home on Sampaguita Street. She cooked sinigang, adobo, and champorado for her neighbors, charging just enough to cover the ingredients.

Word spread fast. By the end of that first month, the whole barangay knew about the woman on Sampaguita Street whose food tasted exactly like your lola's. People came not just for the food but for the feeling — the warmth of a kitchen that was always open, a table that always had room.

Over the decades, Lola Nena's small carinderia grew into a neighborhood institution. Her daughter Ana joined her in the kitchen at 16. Her grandson Kuya Marco learned the recipes by heart before he finished high school. Each generation added their own touch — but nothing changes the core: fresh ingredients, honest cooking, and food made with genuine love.

Lola Nena still oversees the kitchen today, making sure every pot is seasoned right and every plate goes out with the same care she put into that very first bowl of sinigang in 1987.

Ang Pamilya

Meet the family.

Three generations, one kitchen, one purpose.

Lola Nena

Founder & Head Cook

Estrellita "Nena" Reyes started it all in 1987. At 72, she still oversees every pot and checks every plate before it leaves the kitchen. Her sinigang is legendary. Her leche flan makes grown men emotional.

Ana Reyes

Co-Cook & Manager

Lola Nena's daughter and right hand. Ana learned to cook at 16 and has been running the daily operations for 20 years. She handles the silog meals and introduced halo-halo to the menu in 2003.

Kuya Marco

Kitchen & Takeout

Ana's son and Lola Nena's proud grandson. Marco handles takeout orders and helps prep from 5AM. He memorized all 12 of Lola's core recipes at 15, and yes, he cooks adobo as good as the two women will admit.

What We Stand For

Three things that
haven't changed.

The same rules since Lola Nena lit that first fire in 1987.

01 — Fresh

Fresh Every Day

We buy from the palengke every morning. No frozen ingredients, no shortcuts, no reheated food. If it's not fresh, it doesn't go on the plate — that rule has held since 1987 and it isn't changing.

02 — Homemade

Truly Homemade

Every sauce, every marinade, every serving made from scratch in this kitchen. No commercial mixes, no instant packets. Just recipes handed down by hand, tasted by memory, and adjusted by feel.

03 — Community

For the Barangay

This isn't just a restaurant. It's a table for the neighborhood. We price fairly so everyone can eat well, we keep the carinderia open on holidays, and we've never turned away a hungry neighbor.
